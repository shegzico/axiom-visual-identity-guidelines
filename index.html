Identity guidelines
v1.0

This document defines how the Axiom visual identity is applied. It covers the core elements of the brand system — logo, color, typography, photography, iconography, and motion. It provides clear direction for using them with consistency and care. These guidelines exist not to limit creativity, but to ensure that every expression of Axiom carries the same seriousness and intention that the Fellowship itself demands.

01
Strategy

Memorable brands are built from the inside, out. Rooted in ideas around what our company stands for, it’s this vision that drives our visual identity, who we are and what we believe in.

Purpose

Axiom exists to deliberately form people, not simply to fund them.

In a context where recognition often follows visibility rather than contribution, and where intellectual and civic pursuits are treated as optional, Axiom exists to restore seriousness to what shapes culture over time. We believe that excellent people, placed in misaligned systems, will drift, not because of a lack of talent, but because the incentives around them reward the wrong things.

Axiom’s purpose is to create the conditions in which intellectual discipline, responsibility, and care for others can take root early, be sustained over time, and become the basis for meaningful contribution.

Vision

We envision a culture in which intellectual seriousness and civic responsibility are not exceptional traits, but expected standards.

Axiom’s long-term vision is a society shaped by people who have been formed early to think clearly, act responsibly, and take ownership of the spaces they inhabit. We are committed to the slow work of culture-building, not through moments or statements, but through small groups of people held to high standards over long periods of time.

Our vision is not scale for its own sake, but endurance: institutions, communities, and norms that outlast individuals and trends.

Mission

Axiom’s mission is to support exceptional university students through a sustained, structured fellowship that pairs educational funding with clear expectations for conduct, contribution, and accountability.

We begin at the undergraduate level because formation matters most at the start. By providing long-term support across a student’s university journey, Axiom is able to observe, guide, and shape not just academic performance, but habits of thinking, responsibility, and care.

Through this exchange, support offered in return for stewardship — Axiom seeks to cultivate people who actively contribute to intellectual life and take responsibility for the communities and institutions around them.

Position Statement

Axiom is not a scholarship, and it is not a short-term program.

It is a five-year undergraduate fellowship designed around the idea of stewardship, where funding is treated not as a reward, but as a responsibility entrusted to individuals willing to uphold higher standards over time.

Unlike conventional scholarships that focus primarily on financial need or academic output, Axiom is concerned with formation: how people think, how they act, and how consistently they show up for others. Our position is clear — culture is shaped by people who are supported early, held accountable, and expected to contribute beyond themselves.

02
Logo

The Axiom logo is a foundational expression of the Fellowship’s values and intent. It represents seriousness, responsibility, and long-term commitment to intellectual and civic formation. As the most visible marker of the Axiom identity, the logo must be applied with care, consistency, and respect across all contexts.

Wordmark

The Axiom wordmark is precise and deliberately engineered. The wordmark is designed to communicate seriousness and clarity, avoiding decorative expression. The Axiom wordmark is the primary visual identifier of the Fellowship. It exists as a standalone typographic expression and is never used in combination with the symbol.

Brandmark

The Axiom symbol is a constructed form built around balance and tension. The symbol is intentionally minimal and geometric, allowing it to function clearly at small and large scales. It may be used independently in contexts where Axiom is already established or where the symbol acts as a quiet marker of presence rather than identification.

Lockup

The Axiom lock-up is comprised of two parts, the brandmark and the brand name in the primary typeface. This is used only in select instances where Axiom has no existing recognition. Once familiarity is established, the symbol alone should be preferred.

Exclusion Zone

The exclusion zone ensures the legibility and impact of the logo by isolating it from competing visual elements such as text, graphics and imagery. The diagrams show the correct amount of space that should surround the logo. No accompanying text or logos should appear in this area.

Identity on Color

Always ensure there is enough contrast between the identity elements and background. On dark colors, use it primarily in light colors and on light colors use it primarily in dark. See the color section for more detailed information.

Scaling

The logo has been carefully crafted to read well, even at small sizes. There is no limit at large scale, but be careful at smaller sizes. If legibility is an issue, it’s too small. Recommended minimum size is 20 pixels for screen, and 1/4 inch in print.

Don’ts

Do not diminish the value of the logo in our brand. Avoid the following treatments.

Do not stretch or distort
Do not outline
Do not rotate
Do not use unapproved colors
Do not apply shadows or effects
Do not modify or combine symbol and wordmark
Do not mix color applications
Do not adjust weight or spacing
Do not use low contrast combinations

Typography

Typography is a core part of our visual identity, it gives a voice to our words and reinforces the personality of the Axiom brand.

Primary Typeface

PolySans is a cheeky response on mid 20th century classics. It keeps one foot firmly in tradition, but adds a soft-edge inktrap twist that quietly sets it apart.

Secondary Typeface

Suisse Works is more sophisticated than the traditional serif typefaces of former time, though, with smoother, consistent details and balanced proportions across all styles. It is used to emphasize and call attention in select contexts.

Hierarchy

The Axiom type hierarchy establishes a clear, consistent structure for all communications. Each level — Headline, Subhead, and Body — has a defined role. Deviating from this structure, mixing weights arbitrarily, or introducing additional levels weakens the system. Use the hierarchy as defined, and let the contrast between levels do the work.

Color

Color is used with restraint. The Axiom palette is built for clarity and longevity, not trend. Black and white carry the system; accent colors are introduced with intention and precision.

Primary Palette

Black and white are the foundation of the Axiom visual system. They carry the majority of the brand’s visual weight across all applications and should always be the first choice when selecting a color for any element.

05
Photography

Photography plays a deliberate role in the Axiom visual identity system. When used with intention, it helps communicate the seriousness, care, and human responsibility at the centre of the Fellowship.

Style

Axiom photography prioritizes authenticity over polish. Images should feel observational — as though the camera arrived quietly and captured something real. Avoid staged, overly lit, or stock-feeling compositions.

Preferred subjects include people in thought, in conversation, or in focused work. Spaces that feel lived-in, institutional, or quietly formal. Moments of transition or attention, not performance.

Tone & Treatment

Photography should lean toward desaturated, measured tones. Avoid heavy filters, high-saturation edits, or dramatic color grading. A restrained, film-like quality is preferred.

Black-and-white photography is acceptable and encouraged when context calls for formality or historical resonance. Where color is used, it should support rather than compete with the surrounding brand palette.

Composition

Images should use generous negative space where possible. Subjects should not fill the frame, to leave room for text overlays when needed.
